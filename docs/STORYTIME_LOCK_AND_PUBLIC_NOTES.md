Storytime Lock And Public Notes

STORYTIME_LOCK.md
Status: LOCKED
Date: 2024-10-26

StoryTime is intentionally frozen.

This lock exists to protect the emotional integrity of the system, the trust of families, and the long-term coherence of the experience.

WHAT IS INCLUDED IN THE LOCK
Child Experience
Story structure and pacing

Forest visuals and motion rules

Audio behavior (voice, silence, endings)

Night mode behavior

Failure-mode silence

System Behavior
Story template loader

Server-side voice generation + caching

Firestore data boundaries

No analytics, no tracking, no metrics

Parent Experience
Setup flow language

Consent framing

Absence of dashboards, reports, or summaries

WHAT MUST NOT CHANGE
Stories must not instruct, teach, or resolve

No personalization based on behavior

No rewards, streaks, or progress markers

No questions asked of the child

No interpretation of emotional states

No escalation of intensity

If any of the above changes, StoryTime is no longer StoryTime.

WHAT IS ALLOWED DURING THE LOCK
Writing new stories (content-only)

Fixing bugs that break silence or calm

Infrastructure maintenance

No new concepts may be introduced.

REVIEW WINDOW
StoryTime may only be reviewed for change after extended real-world use and only if the criteria in the Forward Criteria document are met.

Until then, the correct action is restraint.

ONE-PAGE PUBLIC EXPLANATION
What StoryTime Is
StoryTime is a quiet place for stories.

It offers slow, repeating narratives that end in calm. Stories are not personalized, measured, or optimized. Silence is allowed. Nothing needs to be learned or completed.

StoryTime is designed to be returned to gently, night after night, without pressure or performance.

What StoryTime Is Not
StoryTime is not an educational program. It does not track attention, behavior, or progress. It does not ask children questions about themselves. It does not try to improve, fix, or accelerate development.

What Parents Should Expect
Familiar stories told slowly

Calm endings

No scores, streaks, or rewards

No summaries or reports

If StoryTime ever feels rushed, loud, or demanding, something has gone wrong.

Why StoryTime Exists
StoryTime exists to witness a child, not shape them.

It is meant to be a steady presence — something that can exist alongside family life without competing for attention.

CRITERIA FOR MOVING FORWARD
StoryTime must remain unchanged unless all of the following are true:

Time Has Passed
StoryTime has been used unchanged for months, not weeks.

Trust Is Evident Without Prompting
Families return to StoryTime naturally, without reminders or encouragement.

No One Asks for Features
The absence of requests is a signal of sufficiency.

Silence Is Accepted
Endings that lead to quiet are not resisted or filled.

Boredom Feels Safe
Repetition creates comfort, not restlessness.

Only if all five conditions are met may the system be considered for gentle expansion — and even then, only one inch at a time.

StoryTime is complete by design.

Growth is optional. Trust is not.
