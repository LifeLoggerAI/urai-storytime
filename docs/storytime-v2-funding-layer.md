# Storytime V2 - Grant & Donation Funding Layer

This document outlines the strategy for securing grants and donations to support the Foundation-backed free tier of Storytime V2.

## Positioning

Storytime will be positioned not as a "kids' app," but as a **"digital tool for emotional regulation and literacy in early childhood."** This framing is critical for attracting institutional funding.

## Target Grant Areas

1.  **Mental Health Foundations:** Focus on the "calm ritual" and "emotional regulation" aspects of the morning and night modes.
2.  **Educational Equity Programs:** Highlight the free tier's role in providing high-quality, safe, educational content to underserved communities.
3.  **Child Literacy Initiatives:** Emphasize the narrative and vocabulary-building components of the stories.
4.  **Tech Ethics & Digital Wellness Research:** Position Storytime as a case study in non-extractive, non-addictive design for children.

## Funding Logic

- **Free Tier as Impact:** The free tier provides a clear, measurable outcome for funders: the number of children served.
- **Commercial Tier as Sustainability:** The paid tier, run by URAI Labs, ensures the long-term financial health of the project, making the Foundation's role more focused and efficient.
- **Earmarked Donations:** Allow individual donors to contribute to specific story packs or translations, giving them a tangible sense of impact.

## Cost Structure

The operational costs for the free tier are intentionally low:

- **No Live AI:** Stories are pre-generated, static assets.
- **Minimal Infrastructure:** Cloud Storage and basic Cloud Functions are inexpensive.
- **No Ad-Tech Overhead:** No need for complex and costly ad-serving infrastructure.

This low-cost structure makes the project highly attractive to funders, as their contributions go directly to content and access, not to heavy operational overhead.
