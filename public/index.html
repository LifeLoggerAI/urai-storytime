## What StoryTime Is StoryTime is a quiet place for stories. It offers slow, repeating narratives that end in calm. Stories are not personalized, measured, or optimized. Silence is allowed. Nothing needs to be learned or completed. StoryTime is designed to be returned to gently, night after night, without pressure or performance. ## What StoryTime Is Not StoryTime is not an educational program. It does not track attention, behavior, or progress. It does not ask children questions about themselves. It does not try to improve, fix, or accelerate development. ## What Parents Should Expect - Familiar stories told slowly - Calm endings - No scores, streaks, or rewards - No summaries or reports If StoryTime ever feels rushed, loud, or demanding, something has gone wrong. ## Why StoryTime Exists StoryTime exists to witness a child, not shape them. It is meant to be a steady presence — something that can exist alongside family life without competing for attention.
